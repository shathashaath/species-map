Endangered Species Map

American Buffalo

Bison bison bison

530,000 bison exist on private and public lands. Roughly 15,000 bison roam free of fencing.

Short-tailed Chinchilla

Chinchilla chinchilla

Between 1895 and 1921 over three million chinchilla pelts were exported from Chile.

Dwarf lemur

Allocebus trichotis

Less than 1,000 individuals are thought to survive. They are threatened by habitat loss.

little spotted cat

Leopardus tigrinus

In the Amazon, population density is very low, expected at approximately 0.01/100 km²

Argali

Ovis ammon

On the Tibetan plateau, Schaller (1998) considered that “…the total number of Tibetan argalis could be as low as 7,000”.

Monk Seal

Monachus

Overall, the best total population estimate for 2012 was 1,153 seals (1,015 in the NWHI and 138 in the MHI).

Dhole

Dhole

populations are decreasing and estimated at fewer than 2,500 adults.

Sand Cat

Felis margarita

11 cats were caught in the study area of 15 x 25 km (375 km²)

Numbat

Myrmecobius fasciatus

The population is estimated to be fewer than 1,000 individuals.

Blue Whale

Balaenoptera musculus

Presently, there are an estimated 5-10,000 blue whales in the Southern Hemisphere, and only around 3-4,000 in the Northern Hemisphere.
